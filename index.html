Skrting through rap lyrics of top Vietnamese hip hop artists

Adlibs in hiphop are words or phrases that rappers add to the end of a line to highlight, emphasise or emote their lyrics. They've a distinguishing feature of trap-based hiphop originating from Atlanta and Southern states. Take the Migos, who are well know for echoing a word or theme at the end of their, or within lines. For example, in their breakout song 'Bad and Boujee' opens with 'Raindrop (Drip), drop-top (Drop-top) Smokin' on cookie in the hotbox (Cookie)' This breaks up the flow, creating their iconic triplet flow which made its way across some oceans to the Vietnamese rap scene.
